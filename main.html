Kyoto, Japan


Kyoto, a city on the island of Honshu, was the capital of Japan for over a thousand years. Known for its classical Buddhist temples, as well as gardens, imperial palaces, Shinto shrines, and traditional wooden houses, Kyoto is a place where you can experience the rich cultural heritage of Japan.

  1. Fushimi Inari Shrine - Famous for its thousands of vermilion torii gates.
  2. Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion) - A stunning Zen Buddhist temple covered in gold leaf.
  3. Arashiyama Bamboo Grove - A scenic forest of towering bamboo stalks.
  4. Nijo Castle - A historic castle known for its beautiful gardens and nightingale floors.
  5. Gion District - The most famous geisha district in Kyoto, known for its traditional wooden machiya houses.
  • Kaiseki - A traditional multi-course Japanese dinner.
  • Yudofu - A hot tofu dish popular in Kyoto, especially in winter.
  • Matcha - High-quality green tea powder used in tea ceremonies.
  • Kyoto-style sushi - Known for its unique flavors and presentation.
  • Yuba - Tofu skin, a delicacy often served fresh or in various dishes.
